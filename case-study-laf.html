UX Case Study.

LA Fitness.

Gym chain with more than 700 clubs across the United States.

Background.

LA Fitness is a gym chain with more than 700 clubs across the United States. Their mission is to “provide the means and motivation for people to get as fit as they can.”

Scope.

The primary goal with their app is improve the overall gym experience by reducing the friction for members to access clubs and gym information.

As someone who exercises regularly at LA Fitness, I decided to set out and uncover the pain points behind the mobile app, and find any potential drop-offs where there could be opportunity.

I will be focused on the users' access to membership cards, class reservations, cancelations, and discoverability of local gyms and class schedules.

User Research.

I conducted user research on participants who had never used the app before and had them do simple tasks:

  1. Access membership card
  2. Find local gym
  3. View details for upcoming cycling class
  4. Add local gym and cycling class to favorites
  5. Reserve spot for cycling class
  6. Cancel reservation for cycling class

Results.

Below are the main takeaways from the research:

  • 6 / 10 did not locate the membership card icon on the homepage at a glance
  • 5 / 10 had difficulty adding a gym and/or class to their favorites
  • 4 / 10 could not figure out how to cancel reservation starting from the home screen
  • 3 / 10 did not spot the reserve button at a glance

The Process.

After reviewing each participant’s experience with the app, I proceeded by:

  1. Identifying the frustrations
  2. Prioritizing the issues to be tackled
  3. Mapping out the user flow when it came to searching, reserving, and favoriting a class

This helped me get a better sense of where along the flow there were problems and if there was any relationship between them.

User Pain Points.

As the research results inferred, there were several issues involving the membership card, favoriting, and reservations.

  • Accessing Membership Card: Users were unable to find the membership card icon at a glance on the home screen of the app.
  • Favoriting Gyms and Classes: There was difficulty in figuring out how to add a gym and/or class to their favorites as the call-to-action is currently unclear.
  • Making Reservations: Some users tapped on the “Reserve Now” text in the class list believing that it would sign them up for the class; however, they realized that they had to officially reserve a spot in the Class Details section.
  • Canceling Reservations: Some users had trouble finding the class reservation page from the homepage, which made it difficult for them to cancel reservations.

User Flow.

Before

I mapped out the current user flow to pinpoint the issues from searching for classes and gyms to attending the reserved session. This clarifies where the issues occur during the process.

After

After noting the pain points, I redesigned the user flow with solutions along with the introduction of saving a primary gym location for make the process simpler for the next use.

Solutions.

Membership Card: Users were unable to find the membership card icon at a glance on the home screen of the app.

Favorite: There was difficulty in figuring out how to add a gym and/or class to their favorites as the call-to-action is currently unclear.

Reservation: Some users tapped on the “Reserve Now” text in the class list believing that it would sign them up for the class; however, they realized that they had to officially reserve a spot in the Class Details section.

Cancelations: Some users had trouble finding the class reservation page from the homepage, which made it difficult for them to cancel reservations.
