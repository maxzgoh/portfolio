UI Case Study.

Department of Homeland Security.

The U.S. federal government department on a mission to enhancing the security of the country.

Scope.

With a plethora of information and topics handled by the DHS, I sought to improve the architecture, brand, and design of their site to allow increase usage, favorability, and ease-of-use for visitors. My main focus was the navigation menu, interface, and content strategy.

Brand Voice.

Our objective was to refresh the brand by highlighting the voice of strength, security, and responsibility while introducing additional undertones of simplicity and modernity through redesigning the website. I wanted to provide clean, purposeful, and modern design to increase consideration, usage, and overall satisfaction for users of all age groups.

Site Map.

I created a site map with multiple buckets of information that would make it easier for users to navigate and find information that they are looking for.

Red-lining.

I also redlined the homepage to understand the dimensions and content strategy behind the existing website for desktop, mobile, and tablet.

Wireframes.

After resorting the information architecture and redlining the homepages, I drafted wireframes for all devices with the goal making information clearer and more accessible for users.

User Test Plan.

We set up a user test plan after designing the initial wireframes and set up the following test objectives:

  • To determine design inconsistencies and usability problem areas within the navigation menu
  • To exercise website under controlled test conditions with participants
  • To establish baseline user performance and user-satisfaction levels of the user interface for future usability evaluations.
  • To identify potential design concerns to improve the efficiency, productivity, and end-user satisfaction.

Style Guide.

Once we completed the user testing and wireframes based on the results, we then created a style guide to keep the interface elements consistent.

Hi-Fidelity Prototypes.

When the style guide was finalized, we applied the style onto the wireframes and created a clickable prototype.
